Through The Years
FRANCHISE
HISTORY
1977
Birth of the Mariners
The Seattle Mariners join the American League as an expansion team. The Pacific Northwest finally has Major League Baseball. A new chapter begins in the Emerald City.
1989
The Kid Arrives
Ken Griffey Jr. makes his debut at 19 years old and immediately transforms the Mariners. His legendary swing and electric personality make Seattle must-watch baseball.
1995
THE DOUBLE — A City Saved
"The Double!" Edgar Martinez hits a walk-off against the Yankees, sending the M's to the ALCS and saving baseball in Seattle. One of the most iconic moments in MLB history.
2001
116 Wins — All-Time Record Tied
Ichiro wins ROY and MVP. The Mariners tie the 1906 Cubs with 116 regular season wins — the most ever in the modern era. An absolutely legendary season.
2022
End of The Drought
After 21 years — the longest playoff drought in North American pro sports — the Mariners return to the postseason. Cal Raleigh's walk-off homer seals it. Seattle explodes.
2025
AL West Champions + ALCS
The Mariners win their first division title since 2001 and advance to the American League Championship Series. A new golden era begins in the Pacific Northwest.
2026
50th Season — World Series Year?
In their 50th anniversary season, the Mariners boast one of the deepest rosters in franchise history. The World Series is the goal. The time is now. Seattle is ready.
